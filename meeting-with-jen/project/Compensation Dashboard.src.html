Assumptions driving the numbers

  • Current compensation package is $158,990 — salary ($110k) + benefits ($25.85k) + travel ($22.9k). Tools ($6.2k) currently out of pocket.
  • Baseline hourly rate of $57/hr reflects actual hours worked, not a 40-hour week.
  • Contract rate markup of 30% covers no benefits, self-employment overhead, and no PTO.
  • Model 1 contractor absorbs $8–10k/yr in self-paid benefits; $10k used in the net figure above.
  • Model 2 benefits remain at 23.5% of salary per current plan.
  • Model 3 travel uses a custom standard rather than full actuals — $40/day meals, $200–250/round trip for gas.
  • All figures in CAD; changes in the controls apply to every view instantly.

Read this before deciding

Model 1 is a different relationship Moving to contract means no PTO, no statutory protections, and the employee carries benefits + tax risk. The $70/hr rate is the floor that makes this neutral for the individual.
Model 2 is the simplest administratively One employment contract, one benefits plan, KCA owns the travel spend. Highest employee stability; middle KCA cost.
Model 3 stops the leak, doesn't change the wage Smallest change for KCA. Stops the employee absorbing $22.9k of operational costs — but doesn't address the hours-vs-salary gap.
